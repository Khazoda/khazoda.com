### Your first step is to gather tin and copper
#### Tin Gear
Tin is a sidegrade to copper, being generally *more effective* but *less durable*.  
Tin weapons and armour are crafted with **`Tin Ingots`**, which are easily obtained by smelting **`Raw Tin`**.

#### Bronze Gear
Bronze is a sidegrade to iron, having *mostly the same* stats but *greater durability*.  
You will need 1 **`Raw Tin`** and 3 **`Raw Copper`** for every **`Bronze Ingot`** you
want to make.<br/>Craft these items together in a crafting window to
obtain **`Bronze Blend`** - then smelt this item in a furnace to obtain a **`Bronze
Ingot`**.

### Tin Armor

Tin is a sidegrade to copper, with some distinct differences:
- Tin armor's durability is exactly halfway between leather and copper
- Tin chestplates provide `+1 Armor` over copper
- Tin armor has 1 more enchantability than copper
- Supports armor trims

### Bronze Armor
<img style="float: right;" src="https://cdn.modrinth.com/data/cached_images/346e2a3da0731a973bec88f88f9048afecc38057.png" alt="Trimmable bronze armor items">

Bronze is a sidegrade to iron, with some distinct differences:
- Bronze armor's durability is exactly halfway between iron and diamond
- All bronze armor pieces have
  [`+1 Armor Toughness`](https://minecraft.wiki/w/Armor#Armor_toughness).
- Supports armor trims

### Sickle

<img style="float: right;" src="https://cdn.modrinth.com/data/cached_images/a4b2220feb0f83019970c8de407dd0ef79554e5b.png" alt="Sickle item">

This mod adds a new tool in the same classification as Shears - the **`Sickle`**.<br>
This item helps you with the crops and flora in your world. It has different functions depending on *how* you interact, and *what* you interact with.
All these interactions are performed in an area with a configurable radius of 1-10, with the default being 4 blocks.
- Right Click
	- Crops: Harvests and replants fully grown crops, with a low chance per crop to give bonus useful items
	- Flowers: Plucks all flowers in the area, leaving everything else intact
- Left Click:
	- Grass: Cuts down all grass-like blocks, including tall grass, ferns etc. This action leaves flowers and other blocks intact.

**Adding blocks to tags**  
You can add blocks to the `#bronze:sickle_mow` and `#bronze:sickle_pluck` block tags to include blocks that the sickle can affect. Crops from other mods should automatically be detected, but if not, please open a bug report on Bronze's [GitHub Repository](https://github.com/Khazoda/bronze/issues).

**Use Case**  
The **`Sickle`** is the perfect item for builders, who want to clear an area of land before building, as well as farmers, who will benefit from faster crop harvests, auto-replanting and bonus item drops.<br/>Drops from blocks destroyed with the sickle are respected, so you will still get all those seeds from the grass you cut down.

![Bronze Mod Future](https://github.com/Khazoda/bronze/blob/Web-Assets/description_common/bronze_future.png?raw=true)
The bronze mod has ~~three~~ two main goals it aims to achieve:

- Flesh out midgame progression for survival Minecraft
- Add simple but useful features that enhance vanilla-centered gameplay
- ~~Give copper a whole new avenue of utility outside of redstone and building~~  (*1.21.10 finally gave copper the update it deserved!*)

#### With this in mind, there are a few new features I'd like to implement in the Bronze mod going forward:

This mod already includes a few non-combat blocks and items, such as the `Bronze Door`,
`Bronze Trapdoor` and `Sickle`, but I believe there's room for more.  
`Update: Since 1.0.11+1.21 - a myriad of new tin-based building blocks have been added.`<br/>
`Update: Since 2.0.4+1.21.10 - Tin armour, tools and weapons have been added`<br/>

Iron ingots have many different roles in vanilla Minecraft. They can be used for armor, weapons,
tools, pistons, chains, pressure plates and about 35 other recipes. While I don't want bronze to be
as useful as iron, as I believe it would take away from iron's mid-game dominance as a useful
material too much, I do want to add some unique features that round the material out.

To simplify this thought process:

- ‚ùå This mod **will not** hijack all of iron's recipes with its own versions, e.g. Bronze Bucket,
  Bronze Shears etc.
- ‚úÖ This mod **will** come up with simple new utilities/blocks for bronze that don't feel out of
  place in the base game e.g. Sickle

Also, much like many of my other mods, I intend to update this mod with each new [*major* version](https://www.khazoda.com/versions) of
Minecraft going forward. If you've read this far, thank you! I hope you enjoy using this mod, and if
you have any queries/issues contact me via the mod's GitHub issues, via Discord or in the CurseForge
comments section üôÇ.
